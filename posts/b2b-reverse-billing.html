Reverse data billing, commonly referred to as zero rating, allows for the provision of complimentary access to specific websites or mobile applications. This method involves commercial enterprises or local governments sponsoring Internet access to their online resources for designated users, either all users or those on a whitelist.

Enhanced Accessibility and Inclusivity:

By offering complimentary access to specific websites or mobile applications, reversed billing or zero rating promotes increased accessibility for users, especially those with limited financial resources. This approach ensures that individuals can easily and affordably access essential online resources, contributing to a more inclusive and equitable digital environment.

Promotion of Online Engagement:

The service encourages higher levels of online engagement by providing free access to designated websites or applications. This strategy not only fosters increased user interaction with the sponsored content but also facilitates the dissemination of important information, promoting educational and informational initiatives among the targeted user base.

Cost-Efficient Marketing and Outreach:

Through the sponsorship of internet access to specific online resources, businesses and local governments can engage in cost-efficient marketing and outreach activities. By leveraging the reversed billing or zero rating service, these entities can effectively promote their products, services, or public information campaigns, reaching a broader audience without imposing additional financial burdens on the users.
